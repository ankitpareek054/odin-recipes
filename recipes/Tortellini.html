Tortellini

Description

Tortellini is a type of stuffed pasta that originated in the Emilia-Romagna region of Italy. It is typically ring-shaped and filled with a mixture of meat, cheese, or vegetables. Tortellini is often served in broth or with a sauce, making it a versatile and delicious dish.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup water (as needed)
  • 1/2 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh spinach (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Make a well in the center and add the egg. Gradually mix the flour into the egg, adding water as needed to form a smooth dough. Knead the dough for about 5-7 minutes until it becomes elastic. Cover with plastic wrap and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. In another bowl, mix together the ricotta cheese, Parmesan cheese, chopped spinach (if using), salt, and pepper until well combined.
  3. Roll out the dough on a floured surface to about 1/8 inch thickness. Cut the dough into 2-inch squares.
  4. Place a small spoonful of the cheese mixture in the center of each square. Fold the square into a triangle, pressing the edges to seal. Bring the two corners of the triangle together and press to form a ring shape.
  5. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the tortellini and cook for about 3-5 minutes or until they float to the surface. Remove with a slotted spoon and serve in broth or with your favorite sauce.
