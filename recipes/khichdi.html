Khichdi

Description

Khichdi is a traditional Indian dish made from rice and lentils, often cooked with spices and vegetables. It is a comforting and nutritious meal that is easy to digest, making it a popular choice for breakfast, lunch, or dinner. Khichdi is often served with yogurt, pickles, or ghee (clarified butter) to enhance its flavor.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 1/2 cup yellow moong dal (split yellow lentils)
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 tablespoon ghee (clarified butter) or oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ginger, grated
  • 1-2 green chilies, chopped (optional)
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, beans, etc.), chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish (optional)

Steps

  1. Rinse the rice and moong dal together under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak them in water for about 15-20 minutes, then drain.
  2. In a large pot or pressure cooker, heat the ghee or oil over medium heat. Add the cumin seeds and let them sizzle for a few seconds until fragrant.
  3. Add the grated ginger and chopped green chilies (if using) to the pot. Sauté for a minute.
  4. Add the chopped mixed vegetables and sauté for 2-3 minutes until they start to soften.
  5. Add the soaked and drained rice and moong dal to the pot. Stir well to combine with the vegetables.
  6. Add turmeric powder and salt to taste. Mix everything together.
  7. Pour in 4 cups of water and bring the mixture to a boil. If using a pressure cooker, cover it with the lid and cook for 3-4 whistles (about 10-15 minutes). If using a regular pot, cover with a lid and simmer on low heat for about 25-30 minutes, or until the rice and dal are cooked and soft. You may need to add more water if it gets too thick.
  8. Once cooked, fluff the khichdi gently with a fork. Garnish with fresh cilantro if desired.
  9. Serve hot with yogurt, pickles, or a dollop of ghee on top for added flavor.
